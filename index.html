About ConGen'25

The next wave of generative AI applications demands models that generate fluent content under strict constraints and rulesâ€”from safety guardrails to compliance with legal, financial, and medical policies. Despite remarkable progress, today's generative models largely operate as stochastic systems, lacking formal guarantees on output behavior. This poses significant risks in safety-critical domains where even minor deviations can lead to incorrect or non-compliant behavior.

This workshop explores research at the intersection of constraint satisfaction, symbolic reasoning, and generative modeling. We bring together speakers from academia, industry, and government to discuss scalable methods for ensuring rule-following behavior in generative AI. Topics include logic-guided decoding, neuro-symbolic integration, formal methods for rule adherence, and evaluation frameworks. Through invited talks, contributed papers, and panel discussions, we aim to chart a research agenda for trustworthy, rule-abiding content generation across domains like code generation, healthcare, finance, and law.

Submission Instructions

Submission Tracks

We welcome paper submissions from all the following tracks.

Track 1: Regular paper submission. This track accepts papers up to 6 pages with unlimited references or supplementary materials.

Track 2: Position paper submission. This track allows papers up to 4 pages to present novel perspectives, future directions, or challenging problems in constraint satisfaction in large generative models.

Submission guidelines and template will be announced soon. The accepted papers are non-archival and non-inproceedings. Concurrent submissions are allowed, but it is the responsibility of the authors to verify compliance with other venues' policies. Based on the PC's recommendation, the accepted papers will be allocated either a spotlight talk or a poster presentation.

Topics

The topics for ConGen'25 include, but are not limited to:

  • Discrete and continuous constraint satisfaction in generative models
  • Constraint-conforming decoding and inference techniques for generative models
  • Scalable structured generation under syntactic and semantic constraints
  • Techniques for satisfying weak and strong constraints in content generation
  • Constraint-guided reasoning
  • Domain specific constraints in language, vision, and multimodal models
  • Human-in-the-loop systems for validating or guiding generation
  • Case studies and deployment lessons from real life applications
